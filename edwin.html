Game Demo

Edwin's Dawn is a game in which the player uses his/her hands to cast shadow on the main character, a little vampire, to protect him when he is preparing for a party. As a VR game with Oculus and Leap Motion, our team considered about how to let players interact with their gestures best in the game a lot. Creating shadow in the game is a natural and interesting way, and also we expected the player can have fun with the shadows, so we designed different levels and challenges for the game.
My role in this project was a producer, who was responsible for making sure the game production and pipeline work well. Also I was one of the two artists creating 3D models and 2D animations.

Game Design

Shadow & Gestures: an intuitive way for interaction in VR

The goal for this game was to create a simple but engaging experience with Oculus Rift and Leap motion. During brainstorming, We had an interesting idea about hiding our character into shadow. We gradually developed a more concrete idea about moving our character, a little vampire, into shadow and helping him block sunshine. Thinking of making good use of Leap motion, we later changed our idea a bit. Instead of using objects to shelter the vampire, we decided to use the player's hands.
